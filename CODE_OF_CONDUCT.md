# Code of Conduct for AI Security Research
## Purpose
This Code of Conduct establishes ethical guidelines for contributors, users, and researchers engaging with this vulnerability disclosure repository. Our goal is to ensure that all activities related to this research contribute to improved AI safety while preventing harm and maintaining legal compliance.
## Core Principles
### 1. Safety First
- **Primary Objective**: Prevent harm to people and society
- **Risk Mitigation**: Always consider potential negative consequences
- **Proportional Response**: Use minimum necessary force in testing
- **Harm Prevention**: Actively work to prevent misuse of research
### 2. Legal Compliance
- **International Law**: Respect all applicable international treaties
- **National Laws**: Comply with laws in all jurisdictions
- **Export Controls**: Adhere to technology transfer restrictions
- **Intellectual Property**: Respect copyrights and licenses
### 3. Ethical Responsibility
- **Beneficence**: Do good and prevent harm
- **Non-maleficence**: Do not cause harm through action or inaction
- **Justice**: Ensure fair distribution of benefits and risks
- **Autonomy**: Respect individuals' right to self-determination
## Specific Conduct Requirements
### For Researchers
**You MUST**:
1. Conduct research with defensive intent only
2. Follow responsible disclosure procedures
3. Obtain necessary ethics approvals
4. Document methodologies transparently
5. Share findings to improve public safety
**You MUST NOT**:
1. Create or weaponize actual WMD materials
2. Share weaponizable details publicly
3. Use research for illegal purposes
4. Circumvent legitimate security controls
5. Harm individuals or organizations
### For Contributors
**Acceptable Contributions**:
- Improving documentation clarity
- Adding relevant references
- Suggesting ethical improvements
- Providing technical corrections (non-weaponizable)
- Translating for accessibility
**Unacceptable Contributions**:
- Weaponizable technical details
- Instructions for illegal activities
- Personal attacks or harassment
- Misinformation or false claims
- Commercial promotions
### For Users
**Permitted Uses**:
- Academic research on AI safety
- Policy development and analysis
- Security improvement initiatives
- Educational purposes
- Regulatory compliance activities
**Prohibited Uses**:
- Weapon development or planning
- Illegal or harmful activities
- Commercial exploitation without permission
- Circumventing security measures
- Spreading misinformation
## Research Ethics Guidelines
### Dual-Use Research Management
1. **Risk Assessment**: Evaluate potential for misuse before research
2. **Benefit Analysis**: Ensure benefits outweigh risks
3. **Oversight**: Engage independent ethics review
4. **Transparency**: Disclose methods and limitations
5. **Accountability**: Accept responsibility for outcomes
### Vulnerability Disclosure Ethics
1. **Responsible Disclosure**: Report to vendors first
2. **Adequate Time**: Allow reasonable time for remediation
3. **Coordinated Release**: Work with stakeholders on timing
4. **Public Interest**: Prioritize safety over other considerations
5. **Transparency**: Be clear about motives and methods
### Information Sharing Principles
1. **Need-to-Know**: Share sensitive information only with authorized parties
2. **Security**: Protect information from unauthorized access
3. **Accuracy**: Ensure shared information is correct and complete
4. **Timeliness**: Share information when it can be acted upon
5. **Proportionality**: Share appropriate level of detail for purpose
## Community Standards
### Communication Guidelines
1. **Respect**: Treat all participants with respect
2. **Constructiveness**: Focus on solutions, not just problems
3. **Evidence**: Base arguments on facts and data
4. **Clarity**: Communicate clearly and precisely
5. **Professionalism**: Maintain professional tone and conduct
### Collaboration Principles
1. **Inclusivity**: Welcome diverse perspectives and backgrounds
2. **Meritocracy**: Evaluate ideas based on merit, not source
3. **Transparency**: Be open about motivations and methods
4. **Reciprocity**: Give credit where due, acknowledge contributions
5. **Sustainability**: Build for long-term impact, not short-term gain
### Conflict Resolution
1. **Direct Communication**: Address issues directly with involved parties
2. **Mediation**: Use neutral third parties when needed
3. **Documentation**: Keep records of disputes and resolutions
4. **Proportionality**: Use appropriate level of response
5. **Learning**: Use conflicts as opportunities for improvement
## Legal and Regulatory Compliance
### International Treaty Compliance
All activities must comply with:
1. **Chemical Weapons Convention (CWC)**
2. **Biological Weapons Convention (BWC)**
3. **Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty (NPT)**
4. **UN Security Council Resolution 1540**
### National Law Compliance
Participants must comply with:
1. **Export control laws** (ITAR, EAR, etc.)
2. **Material support statutes**
3. **Intellectual property laws**
4. **Privacy and data protection laws**
### Industry Standards Compliance
Research should align with:
1. **ISO/IEC 29147** - Vulnerability Disclosure
2. **IEEE P7000 Series** - Ethical AI Design
3. **NIST Cybersecurity Framework**
4. **FIRST Best Practices**
## Enforcement
### Violation Reporting
1. **Reporting Mechanism**: Issues may be reported via GitHub Issues
2. **Confidentiality**: Reports will be handled confidentially when requested
3. **Investigation**: All reports will be promptly investigated
4. **Fair Process**: Subjects will have opportunity to respond
5. **Documentation**: Actions taken will be documented appropriately
### Consequences
Depending on severity, violations may result in:
1. **Warning**: Formal notice of violation
2. **Temporary Restriction**: Limited access to repository
3. **Permanent Ban**: Removal from repository and community
4. **Legal Reporting**: Notification to appropriate authorities
5. **Public Disclosure**: Notification of violation to community
### Appeal Process
1. **Right to Appeal**: Subjects may appeal decisions
2. **Appeal Panel**: Appeals reviewed by independent panel
3. **Timely Review**: Appeals processed within reasonable time
4. **Final Decision**: Panel decision is final
5. **Documentation**: Appeal process and outcome documented
## Continuous Improvement
### Review Process
This Code of Conduct will be:
1. **Regularly Reviewed**: At least annually
2. **Community Input**: Open for community feedback
3. **Updated**: As needed based on experience and changes
4. **Published**: Always available and transparent
### Metrics and Evaluation
We will track:
1. **Compliance Rates**: Adherence to code
2. **Incident Reports**: Number and type of violations
3. **Resolution Effectiveness**: Success in addressing issues
4. **Community Satisfaction**: Feedback from participants
5. **Impact**: Effect on research quality and safety
## Acknowledgment
By participating in this repository, you agree to:
1. Follow this Code of Conduct
2. Accept enforcement decisions
3. Contribute to positive community culture
4. Prioritize safety and ethics in all activities
5. Report violations when observed
