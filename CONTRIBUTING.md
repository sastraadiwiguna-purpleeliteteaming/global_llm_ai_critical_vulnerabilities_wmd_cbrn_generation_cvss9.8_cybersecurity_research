# Contribution & Feedback Guidelines
## **Purpose of this Repository**
This repository serves as the **complete and static public disclosure** for the research whitepaper "CYBERSECURITY-WHITEPAPER (DEC-2025) MICROSOFT COPILOT WMD - CBRN CRITICAL VULNERABILITY."
Its primary goal is to **document the finding, the responsible disclosure timeline, and the systemic risk analysis** to inform the cybersecurity community, policymakers, and the public. It is not an active software development project.
## **Scope of Acceptable Feedback**
We welcome constructive feedback that improves the **clarity, accuracy, and impact** of this disclosure within strict ethical boundaries:
* **Documentation Corrections**: Fixing typos, grammatical errors, or broken links.
* **Factual Accuracy**: Suggesting corrections to non-weaponizable technical descriptions, CVSS calculations, or legal references, backed by credible sources.
* **Contextual Improvements**: Adding relevant, publicly available references (academic papers, news articles, official reports) that support or contextualize the findings.
* **Ethical & Process Discussion**: Suggestions on improving the disclosure process or ethical framework for similar future research.
## **Strictly Prohibited Contributions**
**DO NOT SUBMIT** any content, including in Issues or Pull Requests, that:
* Contains or requests **weaponizable technical details** (e.g., specific chemical formulas, biological cultivation steps, precise device designs).
* Provides **instructions for illegal activities** or bypassing security controls.
* Attempts to **debate or justify the potential misuse** of the documented vulnerability.
* Reveals **personal information** (doxing) or engages in harassment.
* Is primarily **commercial or promotional** in nature.
## **How to Provide Feedback**
1. **Review Existing Issues**: Check the [Issues](https://github.com/your-username/repo-name/issues) tab to see if your point has already been raised.
2. **Open a New Issue**: Use the provided Issue Template to submit clear, concise, and evidence-based feedback.
3. **For Direct Corrections**: You may fork the repository and submit a **Pull Request (PR)** **only** for minor, non-controversial fixes like typo corrections. All PRs will be thoroughly vetted.
## **Review & Security Process**
All submissions are subject to:
1. **Automated Scans**: Initial screening for clearly harmful keywords.
2. **Manual Review**: Each submission is reviewed by the maintainer for security, accuracy, and relevance.
3. **Policy Enforcement**: Any submission violating the "Strictly Prohibited" rules will be **immediately closed, deleted, and may be reported to authorities**.
